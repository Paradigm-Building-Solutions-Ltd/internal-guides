01

Safety & Security.

Claude is a powerful tool. Like any tool, it works best when you use it carefully. The rules below protect employees, clients, and the company. They are short, practical, and easy to follow.

🔒
What you type into Claude is processed by Anthropic.

Never paste information that could expose employees, clients, or the company if it were seen outside PGC. When in doubt, describe the situation in general terms rather than pasting in real names, real numbers, or real documents.

🚫 Never paste any of the following into Claude
✘
Employee personal information
SINs, wages, salaries, medical or disability info, performance ratings, disciplinary records, or home addresses.
✘
Client or customer personal information
Full names combined with contact details, financial information, identification numbers, or purchasing history.
✘
Proprietary or confidential business information
Unreleased product plans, trade secrets, pricing strategies, M&A activity, or details from NDAs and confidential contracts.
✘
Passwords, API keys, or credentials
Never paste login credentials, system tokens, or access keys of any kind.
✘
Sensitive financial data
Bank account numbers, unannounced financial results, audit findings, or detailed budget data with named individuals.
✓ Do
✓Use Claude to draft emails, documents, summaries, and reports.
✓Ask Claude to explain policies, procedures, or technical concepts.
✓Use it to research, brainstorm, or structure your thinking.
✓Always review what Claude produces before sending or acting on it.
✓Describe situations in general terms rather than pasting in real records.
✓Report unexpected or concerning responses to your manager or IT.
✘ Don't
✘Do not paste full employee records, HR files, or performance reviews.
✘Do not share unreleased company plans, contracts, or pricing details.
✘Do not treat Claude's legal, financial, or safety answers as final. Always verify with a person.
✘Do not use Claude to impersonate clients, partners, or employees.
✘Do not share AI-generated content externally without a human review first.
✘Do not stay signed in on a shared computer. Always log out when you are done.
⚠️
One thing to always remember

Claude can sometimes sound very confident while being completely wrong. It can invent names, numbers, citations, dates, and code references that look real but are not. Treat every Claude answer as a first draft. Verify anything factual before relying on it, especially for legal, financial, safety, or client-facing work.

02

Which model should you use?

Inside Claude there are different "models" you can choose from. Think of them like different employees: one is a fast generalist, the other is a senior expert who takes more time but produces better work on hard problems. You pick the right one for the job.

At PGC we use two: Sonnet and Opus. You will see them in the model picker at the top of the chat window. Click the model name to switch.

Everyday Use
Sonnet 4.6
Fast, smart, and gets most things right. Use this for 80% of your work.
Great for
  • Writing emails, memos, and short reports
  • Summarizing meetings, articles, or documents
  • Answering quick questions and explanations
  • Building checklists, agendas, and outlines
  • Light editing and formatting
  • Day-to-day brainstorming and research
Why pick it
  • Replies quickly
  • Uses much less of your usage allowance
  • Quality is excellent for almost any office task
Heavy Lifting
Opus 4.7
The deeper thinker. Slower and uses more allowance, but the smartest model when it matters.
Great for
  • Long, complex documents like proposals, tenders, and reports
  • Multi-step analysis across many files
  • Difficult problems that need careful reasoning
  • Reviewing contracts or technical specifications
  • Strategy work, financial modeling, planning
  • Anything you would normally hand to a senior expert
Why pick it
  • Better at hard reasoning and long context
  • Catches details Sonnet might miss
  • Best for work where mistakes are costly
⚡
Heads up about Opus usage

Opus uses five times more of your weekly and daily allowance than Sonnet. If you use Opus for every task, you will hit your usage limit much sooner and be locked out until it resets. Save Opus for the work that genuinely needs it. For everything else, Sonnet does the job at a fraction of the cost.

💡

Simple rule. Start with Sonnet. If the answer feels shallow, the task is genuinely complex, or you are working on something high stakes, switch to Opus and try again. You can switch mid-chat by clicking the model name.

03

Adaptive thinking.

There is a toggle called Adaptive thinking that tells Claude to slow down and reason through a problem step by step before answering, like a person scribbling on a notepad before speaking. Use it when accuracy matters more than speed.

⚡

Leave it OFF for

Most everyday tasks. Quick questions, drafting an email, summarizing a short doc, brainstorming ideas. You will get faster, more direct answers.

🧠

Turn it ON for

Hard problems that need careful logic. Reviewing a complex contract, working through a tricky calculation, planning something with many moving parts, or any time accuracy matters more than speed.

⚙️
Where to find it: click the model picker at the top of the chat (where you choose Sonnet or Opus). The Adaptive thinking toggle is inside that dropdown. There is also a quick toggle near the send button at the bottom right of the chat input.

Your PGC skills.

"Skills" are like little instruction packs that teach Claude how to do specific kinds of work the way PGC does it. They are already installed and configured on your account. You do not need to turn them on. Just describe what you need, and Claude will pick up the right one automatically.

Paradigm Brand Makes sure documents, emails, social posts, and content follow PGC brand voice, colours, and tone. Works for PGC, Paradigm Solar, Buildforth, and StokedLiving.
Word Documents Creates and edits professional Word files (.docx) with proper formatting, headings, and tables.
Spreadsheets Builds and analyzes Excel files (.xlsx) with formulas, charts, and data tables.
Presentations Turns notes or data into polished slide decks (.pptx).
PDFs Reads, creates, merges, splits, and fills PDF forms.
PDF Reading Pulls text, tables, and content out of PDF files so Claude can work with them.
File Reading Helps Claude understand any uploaded file (Word, Excel, images, archives, and more).

Ask Claude to find the right skill.

Most of the time Claude figures out the right skill on its own. But sometimes a nudge helps, especially when your task could be done in different ways. Adding one short line to your prompt makes a real difference.

Add this to any prompt
"List the skills you have available for this task (including any in the Anthropic skill library you can access), then use the most relevant one before answering."

Or shorter: "Find and use the best skill for this. List what you used at the end." The "list what you used" part is important. It forces Claude to actually check rather than guess, and you can see which skill was applied.

Example: "Build a project budget spreadsheet for the Louis Creek facility upgrade. Find and use the best skill for this and apply PGC branding. List what you used." → Claude loads the Spreadsheets skill and the Paradigm Brand skill, sets Catamaran as the font, names the file properly, uses PGC colours in the charts, and tells you which skills it used.
Example: "Draft a one-page client proposal for StokedLiving. Find and use the right skills." → Claude loads the Word Documents skill and applies the StokedLiving brand voice, colours, and font.
06

Claude Cowork.

Cowork is the part of Claude that does work for you rather than just chats with you. Think of regular Claude as a helpful assistant in a conversation. Cowork is the same assistant, but with hands. It can open your files, read them, edit them, save new ones, browse the web, and run multi-step tasks on its own while you focus on something else.

Where to find it

Open the Claude Desktop App. At the top of the window you will see three tabs: Chat, Cowork, and Code. Click Cowork. That is it. Your computer needs to stay on and the app needs to stay open while Cowork is doing work for you.

📂
Works with your files

You pick a folder on your computer. Cowork can read, edit, and create files inside it. No uploading and downloading.

🔁
Multi-step tasks

You describe the goal in plain English. Cowork plans the steps, shows you the plan, asks before doing anything significant, and works through it.

⏰
Scheduled tasks

Set up a recurring task once. Cowork runs it on schedule. Daily briefings, Monday morning reports, weekly summaries.

👁️
You stay in control

You choose which folders Cowork can touch. It shows what it is about to do before doing it, and you can stop or redirect at any time.

Real PGC examples
"Here is a folder with 30 supplier quotes as PDFs. Build me a comparison spreadsheet with prices, lead times, and a recommended pick." Cowork reads each PDF, pulls the data, builds the spreadsheet, applies PGC branding, and saves it.
"Every Monday at 8am, summarize all the Monday.com tasks that changed last week and email it to me as a one-page brief." A scheduled task. You set it up once. It runs on its own from then on.
"Read the project brief in brief.docx. Search the web for current pricing on the materials listed. Create an updated cost analysis as an Excel file with a written summary." Cowork executes each step in order.
📍
Important to know

Cowork is only in the Desktop app on Mac or Windows. It is not on the website or mobile. You can send instructions from your phone if the Desktop app is paired and running on your computer, and Cowork will execute them there.

Memory & Projects.

These two features make Claude feel less like a stranger every time and more like a coworker who actually remembers what you do.

🧠

Memory

When Memory is on, Claude remembers useful context from past conversations, like your role, your common projects, your writing style, and your preferences. You stop having to re-explain yourself in every chat.

Claude does not store passwords, financial accounts, or sensitive personal details. You can view, edit, or delete any memory at any time. You can also pause it or reset it.

How to turn it on Click your name in the lower-left corner, go to Settings, then Capabilities, then toggle on Generate memory from chat history. You can also enable Search and reference past chats in the same place.
📁

Projects

Projects are shared workspaces where you and your team can work with Claude on the same thing, with the same context and instructions. Upload reference files once, write custom instructions once, and everyone on the project gets the same Claude.

Great for: an HR Project (with policies and templates loaded), a Buildforth Project (with the site brief), a Finance Project (with chart of accounts), or any ongoing work that benefits from shared context.

How to start one In the left sidebar, click Projects, then Create Project. Give it a name, add instructions, upload reference files, then share with colleagues with either "Can use" or "Can edit" permissions.
💡
Tip: Memory is per-user and personal. Projects are shared. Use Memory for your own preferences, Projects for team context.

Smart tips for better answers.

Small habits, big difference in what Claude produces.

💡 Be specific. Tell Claude your role, the context, and what you need the output for.
📝 Name the format. "Write this as a bullet list", "keep it under 150 words", "make it formal".
🎨 Say the brand. For any external or client-facing content, say "this is for PGC" (or Buildforth, Solar, StokedLiving) so the right voice and colours apply.
🔁 Iterate. Ask Claude to refine its own draft. "Make this more concise", "make it less formal", "try a different angle".
🦺 Safety questions. Just ask naturally. "What PPE do I need for working at heights?"
🧠 Hard problem? Switch to Opus and turn on Adaptive Thinking. The wait is worth it.
⚡ Quick task? Stay on Sonnet with thinking off. Faster, lighter, plenty smart.
🔧 Use the right skill. Add "find and use the best skill for this" to the end of any prompt.
⏰ Schedule it. If you do something every Monday, ask Cowork to handle it on a schedule.
👁️ Always review. Claude gives you a first draft, not a final product.
