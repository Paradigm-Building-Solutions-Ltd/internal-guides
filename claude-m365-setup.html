Connect Claude to your Microsoft world.

For Everyone at PGC
Set-up time About 10 minutes
Cost Included in our plan
You need Your PGC work account
The short version

There are two ways to bring Claude and Microsoft 365 together, and they do different jobs.

The Microsoft 365 connector lets Claude search and read across SharePoint, OneDrive, Outlook, and Teams from inside the Claude app. It is read only, so Claude can find and summarize, but never change, send, or delete anything.

Claude in your Office apps adds a Claude panel inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook. There it can read and edit the file you have open, draft text, build slides, and write formulas.

This guide sets up both, one step at a time. No technical knowledge needed.

01
Step one

Connect the Microsoft 365 connector.

BEFORE YOU START Use your work account Sign in with your name@pgc.ca account. Personal Microsoft accounts like @outlook.com or @hotmail.com will not work.

This links Claude to your Microsoft account so it can search your files, email, calendar, and Teams when you ask. You do it once, and it takes about five minutes.

  1. Open your account menu. In Claude, click your name and profile icon at the bottom left of the sidebar.
  2. Click Settings.A settings window opens.
  3. Click Connectors.If you see a message that connectors have moved, click the Customize button to continue.
  4. Find Microsoft 365 in the list.If it is not there, click Add or Browse connectors, then add Microsoft 365.
  5. Click Connect next to Microsoft 365.
  6. Sign in with your PGC work account (your name@pgc.ca email), then review and accept the access request.
That is it. Microsoft 365 now shows as Connected. You can start asking questions right away.
Read only
Claude can search and summarize, but it cannot change, send, or delete anything.
Your permissions apply
Claude only sees what you can already open. Locked files stay locked.
On demand
Claude only looks when you ask a question that needs it. It does not run in the background.

03
Step three (optional)

Add Claude inside your Office apps.

GOOD TO KNOW Needs a paid plan Claude inside Office requires a paid Claude plan (Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise). If you are not sure what you have, ask IT.

This is different from the connector. It puts a Claude panel inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook, where Claude can read and change the file you have open.

  1. Open Word, Excel, or PowerPoint.
  2. Click Add-ins. On Windows it is on the Home tab.On a Mac, use Insert > Add-ins or Tools > Add-ins.
  3. Search for Claude.
  4. Choose Claude for Microsoft 365, not the single-app version.See "Which one to pick" below for why this matters.
  5. Click Add. The Claude panel opens on the right side of the app.
  6. Sign in with your Claude account. Open and sign in once in each app you want to use.
For Outlook. Outlook has its own add-in. Search for and add Claude for Outlook the same way.

Which one to pick

When you search, you will see two results. Pick the first one.

Pick this
Claude for Microsoft 365
One add-in that works in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint together. Claude can carry context across them, for example taking figures from Excel into a PowerPoint slide. Install it once and use it everywhere.
Skip this
Claude for a single app
Works in that one app only. Choose this just if you want Claude in a single program and nowhere else. Most people should not.
Want Claude to move between your open files? Open each add-in's Settings and turn on Let Claude work across files. Then Claude can read your open Excel workbook while it builds your PowerPoint slides.
04
The short version

Connector or Office add-in? When to use each.

Both are useful, in different moments. The connector reaches out to find things. The Office add-in works on the document in front of you.

The connector
Claude in Office
Where it lives
Inside the Claude app (web, desktop, phone).
Inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook.
What it reaches
Your stored content across SharePoint, OneDrive, Outlook, and Teams.
The single file you have open right now.
Can it change things
No. It searches and reads only.
Yes. It can write, edit, and build in the open file.
Best for
Finding, reading, and summarizing across the company.
Drafting, editing, and building documents and slides.
Example
"Find and summarize the Q4 plan in SharePoint."
"Turn these Excel figures into three slides."
Rule of thumb
Looking for something or pulling in context? Use the connector. Building or editing a document? Use Claude in Office. Together they cover the whole job.
And both stay inside your own permissions, so you only ever reach what you could open yourself.
